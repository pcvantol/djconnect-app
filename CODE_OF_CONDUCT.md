# Code of Conduct

DJConnect should be a welcoming, respectful project for everyone who wants to
help build, test, document, or discuss it.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that helps create a healthy community:

- Be kind, patient, and constructive.
- Assume good intent, while still being clear about impact.
- Welcome different experience levels, backgrounds, languages, and viewpoints.
- Give and receive feedback with respect.
- Keep discussions focused on the project and the people using it.
- Respect privacy. Do not share private Home Assistant URLs, tokens,
  diagnostics, personal data, screenshots, or logs without explicit permission.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption.
- Insults, personal attacks, slurs, or demeaning language.
- Sexualized language or imagery in project spaces.
- Publishing or threatening to publish private information.
- Pushing people to reveal credentials, private URLs, or personal data.
- Any other conduct that would reasonably make participation unsafe or
  unwelcome.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in all DJConnect project spaces, including issues,
pull requests, discussions, documentation, release notes, community chat, and
events where someone represents the project.

## Reporting

If you see or experience behavior that violates this code of conduct, report it
to the project maintainer through a private GitHub security advisory or another
private contact channel listed by the project owner.

Please include enough context to understand what happened, such as links,
timestamps, screenshots, or message excerpts. Do not include secrets, tokens,
private Home Assistant URLs, or unrelated personal data.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they consider appropriate and proportionate,
including:

- asking for clarification or an apology;
- editing or removing comments, issues, pull requests, or other contributions;
- temporarily or permanently limiting participation in project spaces;
- reporting serious abuse to the relevant platform or authority.

Maintainers will aim to act fairly, protect affected people, and avoid exposing
private information while handling reports.

## Attribution

This code of conduct is adapted from common open source community guidelines,
including the Contributor Covenant, with privacy and safety language tailored to
DJConnect.
